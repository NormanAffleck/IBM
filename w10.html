Week 10 | Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP)

🏢 What is ERP?

  • Set of integrated software modules and a central database
  • Collects and makes available data across all departments
  • Supports internal business activities end-to-end

🔄 How ERP Systems Work

  • One company, one database — shared across departments
  • Predefined best practice processes for functions like HR, Finance, Sales
  • Firms select modules and adapt their processes accordingly

📈 Business Value of ERP

  • Increased operational efficiency and data visibility
  • Faster customer response and improved decision-making
  • Analytical tools for evaluating performance

🔗 Supply Chain Overview

  • Upstream: Supplier management
  • Downstream: Product distribution
  • Goal: Match supply and demand efficiently

⚙️ Supply Chain Strategies

  • Just-in-time (JIT) vs. Safety stock buffering
  • Bullwhip effect: Small demand changes ripple upstream
  • Push-based vs. Pull-based manufacturing

📊 Supply Chain Software

  • Planning: Demand forecasting, inventory, sourcing
  • Execution: Distribution and warehouse management

🌍 Global Supply Chain Management

  • Challenges: Time zones, legal differences, standards
  • Solutions: Internet-based coordination, outsourcing

❤️ Customer Relationship Management (CRM)

  • Collects, integrates, and distributes customer data
  • Enhances service, sales, and marketing
  • Single enterprise-wide view of customer

🧠 Analytical CRM

  • Uses data warehouses and mining for customer insights
  • Focus on Customer Lifetime Value (CLTV)

📉 Challenges of ERP

  • High costs, long implementation, organizational change
  • Vendor dependence and complex data standardization

🚀 Next-Gen ERP Applications

  • Open-source, cloud, mobile-enabled ERP
  • Social CRM, analytics integration, and BI tools
