Week 2 | Business Principles of MIS

💡 Key Concepts

  • Information Technology (IT): Tools (hardware/software) used to achieve business goals.
  • Information Systems (IS): Interrelated components that collect, process, store, and distribute information to support decision-making and control.
  • Difference Between IT and IS: IS includes people and processes beyond just tech tools.

🔍 Three Dimensions of IS

  • Organizational: Hierarchies, departments, business processes, culture, politics.
  • Managerial: Strategy, leadership, creativity, and organizational response.
  • Technological: Hardware, software, data, networks, IT infrastructure.

🏢 Business Perspective on IS

IS should lead to better decisions, efficiency, and profits. They are not just tech systems but tools to shape business value. A sociotechnical view helps avoid purely technical approaches.

🔄 Business Processes

Defined flows of material, information, and tasks within an organization. IS helps automate and innovate these processes for better efficiency and outcomes.

🌐 E-Business & E-Commerce

  • E-Business: Use of internet and digital tech in core business processes.
  • E-Commerce: Subset of e-business focusing on online transactions.
  • E-Government: Use of internet tech to deliver services to public.

📊 How IS Improve Business

  • Increase efficiency by automating steps
  • Enable new models and parallel workflows
  • Reduce delays and enhance decision-making

⚙️ Types of Systems in Business

  • MIS: Reports for routine and some non-routine decisions
  • DSS: Decision Support for unique, data-driven analysis
  • ERP: Enterprise-wide systems integrating processes and data
  • SCM: Supply Chain tools for logistics and sourcing
  • CRM: Customer relationship tools to boost satisfaction and retention
  • KMS: Knowledge systems for capturing and sharing insights

🚀 What’s New in MIS

  • Cloud computing, mobile platforms, AI, machine learning
  • New business models like streaming and on-demand platforms
  • Rise of social tools, virtual teams, and intelligent dashboards

🌍 Globalization & IS

IS enables international operations, reduces costs, and connects global economies. Companies must now manage data privacy (GDPR, DSA) and explore global talent, markets, and data infrastructure.

🪄 Closing Thought

Business principles in MIS turn static tools into strategic enablers. They’re your blueprint for crafting a smarter, faster, and more adaptive enterprise.
