Week 3 | Revenue Management

📘 Definition

  • Strategies to maximize revenue by controlling demand and supply
  • Price → Demand Management
  • Time & Space → Supply Management

🏭 Characteristics of RM Industries

  • Fixed capacity
  • Perishability
  • Seasonality
  • High fixed cost, low variable cost
  • Market segmentation by price sensitivity

📜 History

  • Started in airline industry after Deregulation Act (1978)
  • Delta & American Airlines → RM pioneers (4–8% revenue increase)
  • Expanded to: hotels, car rentals, spas, theaters, theme parks

📊 Major Roles

  • Forecasting: Demand, no-shows, using time-series or causal analysis
  • Optimization: Price discrimination, overbooking, inventory allocation
  • Market segmentation: Price sensitive vs. insensitive customers
  • Goal: Right product at the right price to the right customers at the right time

🎯 Key Strategic Levers

  • Price: Variable pricing, discounts, rate fences (physical & non-physical)
  • Time: Explicit (per hour/day) or implicit (e.g. golf game, show)
  • Space: Explicit (per square meter), Implicit (room, seat, table)

📈 Industry Examples

  • Hotel: Revenue Per Available Room-Night
  • Restaurant: Revenue Per Available Seat-Hour
  • Rental Car: Revenue Per Available Car-Day
  • Stadium: Revenue Per Available Seat-Game
  • Golf Club: Revenue Per Available Tee-Time
  • Spa: Revenue Per Available Treatment-Hour
