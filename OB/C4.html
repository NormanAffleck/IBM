üòä Emotions vs. Moods

  • Emotions: intense, short-lived, caused by specific events (e.g., anger, fear).
  • Moods: less intense, longer-lasting, not always triggered by events.
  • 6 universal emotions: anger, fear, sadness, happiness, disgust, surprise.
  • Moral emotions: based on ethical judgments; vary across cultures.

üîç Sources of Emotions and Moods

  • Personality: trait-based emotional tendencies (affect intensity).
  • Time: happier mid-day and on weekends.
  • Weather, stress, and social interactions can impact mood.
  • Sleep, exercise, and gender identity also influence emotional state.

üé≠ Emotional Labor

  • Expression of desired emotions during work.
  • Surface acting: fake expressions.
  • Deep acting: modifying internal feelings.
  • Emotional dissonance: mismatch between felt and displayed emotions ‚Üí leads to burnout and reduced job satisfaction.

‚ö° Affective Events Theory (AET)

Workplace events trigger emotional reactions that affect employee attitudes and performance. Managers should pay attention to even minor emotional events.

üß† Emotional Intelligence (EI)

  • Ability to perceive, understand, and regulate emotions.
  • Perceive: recognize emotions in self and others.
  • Understand: interpret emotion causes and meanings.
  • Regulate: manage emotional responses effectively.

üßò Emotion Regulation

  • Recognize and modify emotional responses.
  • Techniques: surface acting, deep acting, suppression, reappraisal, social sharing.
  • Ethical debate: authenticity vs. professionalism in emotional control.

üè¢ Applications in OB

  • Selection: EI is valuable in hiring for interpersonal roles.
  • Decision Making: Positive moods improve decisions.
  • Creativity: Positive emotions boost creative thinking.
  • Motivation: Moods influence motivation via feedback.
  • Leadership: Emotionally aware leaders inspire more.
  • Negotiation: Emotions shape negotiation outcomes.
  • Customer Service: Emotion contagion affects satisfaction.
  • Work-Life: Moods spill between home and work.
  • Deviance & Safety: Negative moods increase risk.
