Chapter 2 | Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion

📚 Learning Objectives

  • Describe the two major forms of workplace diversity.
  • Demonstrate how workplace prejudice and discrimination undermines organizational effectiveness.
  • Explain four major theoretical perspectives on workplace diversity.
  • Describe diversity’s role in interpersonal interactions.
  • Discuss cross-cultural implications for OB.
  • Describe effective diversity management practices.

🌈 Understanding Diversity

  • Surface-level: Gender, age, race, ethnicity
  • Deep-level: Values, personality, preferences

🧬 Biographical Characteristics

  • Age, gender identity, race, ethnicity
  • Glass ceiling, cliff, and sexual orientation

💸 Gender Pay Gap

Though narrowing, pay disparity still exists. (U.S. BLS, 2020)

🚫 Prejudice & Discrimination

  • Prejudice: Broad negative attitudes
  • Implicit bias: Subconscious attitudes
  • Discrimination: Actions reinforcing inequality
  • Disparate impact: Unintended discriminatory effects
  • Disparate treatment: Intentional discrimination

📉 Effects on Organizational Effectiveness

Discrimination undermines fairness, morale, and performance. See Exhibit 2.1.

🧠 Theories on Prejudice & Diversity

  • Social categorization: Ingroups vs. outgroups
  • Stereotyping & stigma: Threats and hidden biases
  • System justification theory & Social dominance theory
  • Intersectionality: Identities interacting (e.g., double jeopardy)

🔀 Diversity Dynamics

  • Group composition: surface/deep/functional diversity
  • Fault lines: subgroup splits causing tension

🌍 Cross-Cultural OB

  • Hofstede: Power distance, individualism, masculinity, etc.
  • GLOBE: 9 dimensions including future orientation, gender equality
  • Cultural tightness-looseness & Religion’s impact
  • Cultural intelligence (CQ): Adaptability in diverse settings
