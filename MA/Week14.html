🚖 Waymo Case Overview

Waymo is a self-driving car company owned by Google’s parent company. Its ride-hailing services were doing over 250,000 rides as of mid-2024, with operations in cities like Phoenix, LA, and San Francisco. Its driverless service is now more popular than traditional attractions.

📊 Growth Milestones

  • 10,000 weekly rides (Aug 2023)
  • 50,000 trips/month (May 2024)
  • 100,000 (Aug 2024)
  • Over 1M rides (late 2023)
  • 5M (end of 2024)
  • 10M+ (mid 2025)
  • Expected 20M by end of 2025

📈 Market Adoption & Inflection Point

Waymo experienced steady growth before a sudden surge in March 2025 (27% rise). This marks an inflection point in consumer adoption—moving from novelty to norm—following the classic technology adoption curve.

🌐 Expansion & Strategy

  • Entered Austin, Texas via Uber
  • Launching in: Atlanta, Miami, DC
  • Mapping: Boston, Nashville, Vegas, San Diego
  • Collecting data in Tokyo

⚔️ Competitors

  • Uber & Lyft: abandoned self-driving R&D
  • GM: scrapped Cruise robotaxis
  • Amazon: owns Zoox
  • Tesla: Main current rival in the U.S.

🔍 Tesla vs. Waymo

Tesla uses cameras + AI, whereas Waymo uses high-cost LIDAR + sensor feedback. Tesla is preparing its own robotaxi launch. Elon Musk quipped that Waymo’s model “costs way-mo money.”

🎯 Conclusion

Waymo stands as a dominant leader in the autonomous taxi race with strong market adoption, rapid scaling, and a clear strategy, despite its high costs and strong competition.
