💡 What is Marketing?

Marketing is a process by which companies create value for customers and build strong customer relationships to capture value from customers in return. It involves creating, communicating, delivering, and exchanging offerings that have value for customers, clients, partners, and society at large.

🌐 Marketing 4.0

  • Combines online and offline interaction
  • Authenticity is crucial in the digital era
  • Brands must be flexible and adaptive while maintaining core identity

🔍 Customer Needs, Wants & Demands

  • Needs: Physical, Social, Individual
  • Wants: Shaped by culture and personality
  • Demands: Wants backed by buying power

📈 Customer Value and Satisfaction

  • Set the right expectations—not too high or low
  • Examples: Wholefoods, SSG Food

🔁 Exchange & Markets

  • Exchange: Getting a desired object by offering something in return
  • Markets: Set of actual and potential buyers

🎯 Designing a Customer-Driven Strategy

  • Market Segmentation and Targeting
  • Examples: Nordstrom, Macy’s, Neiman-Marcus

✅ Conclusion

Chapter 1 emphasizes creating and capturing customer value through deep understanding of customer needs, segmentation, and delivering satisfaction better than competitors. The societal concept adds long-term benefits for society and the firm.
