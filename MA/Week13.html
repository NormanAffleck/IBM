📰 Case Background

Southwest Airlines is introducing new baggage fees to align with the industry trend and increase revenue. The decision marks a shift from their long-standing “bags fly free” policy.

💼 Key Details from the Case

  • Fee Announcement Date: March 2025
  • Effective for bookings from: May 28, 2025
  • New Fees: $35 for the first bag, $45 for the second bag
  • Free for: Credit card holders, elite status members (1st or 2nd tier)
  • Same fee structure as competitors (Delta, United, etc.)

🧭 Strategic Shift

  • Southwest had resisted fees and basic economy to maintain a “customer-friendly” image.
  • The company trademarked the phrase “bags fly free.”
  • This new policy represents a change in brand positioning.

🧩 Implications

  • Potential backlash from loyal customers used to free checked baggage.
  • Shift toward monetizing services previously bundled in base fares.
  • Brand identity may need to adapt with this transformation.
  • Seats with assignments (instead of open seating) are also being introduced.

✅ Conclusion

This case illustrates how even customer-centric brands like Southwest must adapt their strategies in response to competitive and financial pressures—highlighting the delicate balance between loyalty and profitability.
