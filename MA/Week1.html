Introduction to Marketing

Marketing is defined as the activity, set of institutions, and processes for creating, communicating, delivering, and exchanging offerings that have value for customers, clients, partners, and society at large.

Marketing 4.0 Era

Marketing 4.0 represents a transition from traditional to digital marketing, combining online and offline engagement. It emphasizes authenticity, flexibility, and adaptation to rapid technological changes.

Marketing Process

The five-step marketing process includes understanding customer needs, designing a customer-driven strategy, preparing an integrated marketing program, building customer relationships, and capturing value from customers.

Customer Needs, Wants, and Demands

Needs are states of deprivation. Wants are needs shaped by culture and personality. Demands are wants backed by purchasing power.

Customer Value and Satisfaction

Customer value is the perceived benefit versus cost. Marketers must manage expectations—setting them too high risks dissatisfaction, while too low may fail to attract.

Exchanges and Markets

Marketing involves exchange: obtaining a desired object by offering something in return. Markets are actual and potential buyers of a product.

Designing a Customer-Driven Strategy

Target markets are selected through segmentation (e.g., by age, gender, income). A strong value proposition differentiates the company from competitors.

Marketing Management Orientations

There are five orientations: Production, Product, Selling, Marketing, and Societal. Each has a distinct focus—from efficiency to long-term societal good.
