📊 Strategic Planning & Marketing's Role

  • Strategic planning matches goals with marketing opportunities.
  • Mission statements define purpose in satisfying customer needs.
  • Examples: Disney, Kakao Vision.

🎯 Company Objectives & Portfolio Analysis

  • Business Objectives: customer relationships, R&D, profit growth
  • Marketing Objectives: market share, partnerships, promotion
  • Tools: Growth-share matrix (Stars, Cash Cows, Question Marks, Dogs)
  • Portfolio examples: smartphone, detergent brands, outdated software

📈 Growth Strategies

  • Market Penetration: Sell more to current markets
  • Market Development: Find new markets for existing products
  • Product Development: New products for current markets
  • Diversification: New products for new markets
  • Downsizing: Cut unprofitable units (e.g. GM, Ford, IBM examples)

🤝 Partnering Across Company & Beyond

  • Value Chain: Departments creating value together
  • Value Delivery Network: Company + suppliers + distributors + customers

🧠 Customer-Driven Marketing Strategy

  • Segmentation: Divide market by characteristics
  • Targeting: Select which segments to enter
  • Differentiation: Make product stand out
  • Positioning: Place in customer’s mind (e.g. BMW, Samsung)

📦 Marketing Mix (4Ps → 4Cs)

  • Product → Customer Solution
  • Price → Customer Cost
  • Place → Convenience
  • Promotion → Communication

🛠 Managing the Marketing Effort

  • Marketing Analysis: SWOT (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats)
  • Marketing Planning: Budget, Strategy, Action Programs
  • Marketing Implementation: Who, Where, When, How

💹 ROI and Performance

  • Return on Marketing Investment (ROI): profit from investment / cost
  • Used to measure effectiveness of marketing efforts

✅ Conclusion

Chapter 2 explores how strategic planning aligns with marketing operations, growth strategies, the marketing mix, and the implementation of effective, ROI-focused campaigns.
