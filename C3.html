üß† Components of an Attitude

Attitudes are evaluative statements about people, objects, or events. They have three components:

  • Cognitive: Opinion or belief segment.
  • Affective: Emotional or feeling segment.
  • Behavioral: Intention to behave in a certain way.

üîÅ Attitudes and Behavior

Cognitive dissonance occurs when there‚Äôs inconsistency between attitudes or between attitudes and behavior. People seek consistency and reduce dissonance by changing attitudes, modifying behavior, or rationalizing.

  • Moderators: attitude importance, correspondence to behavior, accessibility, social pressures, personal experience.

  • Organizational Identification ‚Äî alignment with organizational characteristics.
  • Job Satisfaction ‚Äî positive feelings about one‚Äôs job.
  • Job Involvement ‚Äî psychological identification with the job.
  • Psychological Empowerment ‚Äî influence, autonomy, and meaningfulness.
  • Organizational Commitment ‚Äî emotional attachment to the organization.
  • Perceived Organizational Support ‚Äî belief that the organization cares and values contributions.
  • Employee Engagement ‚Äî enthusiasm and involvement in work.

üìä Measuring Job Satisfaction

  • Single global rating: one overall question.
  • Summation of job facets: detailed breakdown of satisfaction elements.

üìà Causes of Job Satisfaction

  • Job conditions: nature of work, social interaction, supervision.
  • Personality: positive core self-evaluation leads to higher satisfaction.
  • Pay: correlates with satisfaction up to a point.

üéØ Outcomes of Job Satisfaction

  • Job performance: satisfied workers are more productive.
  • OCB (Organizational Citizenship Behavior): discretionary effort.
  • Customer satisfaction: satisfied employees yield happier customers.
  • Life satisfaction: positively correlated with job satisfaction.

üö® Responses to Dissatisfaction

  • Exit: leaving the organization.
  • Voice: actively trying to improve conditions.
  • Loyalty: passively waiting for improvement.
  • Neglect: allowing conditions to worsen.

üõë Counterproductive Work Behavior (CWB)

Dissatisfied employees may engage in theft, lateness, or absenteeism. Turnover is often linked to low satisfaction. Managers must monitor and address dissatisfaction proactively.
