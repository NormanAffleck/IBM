üëÅÔ∏è Perception

Perception is the process by which individuals interpret sensory input to understand their environment. People act based on their perception of reality, not necessarily reality itself.

üß† Attribution Theory

  • Explains how we determine if a behavior is internally or externally caused, based on distinctiveness, consensus, and consistency.
  • Common errors: Fundamental Attribution Error and Self-Serving Bias.
  • Shortcuts: Selective perception, Halo effect, Contrast effect, Stereotyping.
  • Applications in hiring, evaluations, and expectations (e.g., Pygmalion effect).

üìå Decision Making

  • Decision: a choice among alternatives made in response to a problem.
  • Rational Model: Logical six-step model based on full information and utility maximization.
  • Bounded Rationality: We simplify complex problems and satisfice instead of optimizing.
  • Intuition: A fast, emotionally driven decision style outside conscious thought.

üß© Biases & Remedies

  • Biases: Overconfidence, anchoring, confirmation, hindsight.
  • Remedies: Set clear goals, seek disconfirming info, avoid randomness, increase options.

üí° Risk & Individual Differences

  • Risk aversion: Preferring sure outcomes, especially under stress or threat.
  • Differences: Personality, intuition, self-esteem, gender, mental ability, culture.
  • Constraints: Evaluations, rewards, regulations, time, history.
