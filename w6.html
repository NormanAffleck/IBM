Week 6 | Mobile Business

📲 Mobility: The Business Value of Wireless

  • Pervasive computing enables real-time data collection and interaction via smart devices
  • Examples: smartwatches, smart speakers, and always-on devices
  • Concerns: device loss, data breaches, and BYOD (Bring Your Own Device) risks

📋 Enterprise Mobility Management (EMM)

  • Helps enforce security policies across mobile devices
  • Manages apps, data, and devices both inside and outside the workplace
  • Prevents unauthorized access to corporate systems

📱 Mobile Device Management (MDM)

  • Remote support and control over corporate devices
  • Supports BYOD, CYOD, and COPE strategies
  • Enables remote data wiping and access control

🔒 Mobile Application Management

  • Focuses on encrypting data and managing access by apps
  • Supports fast data: real-time analytics on smaller data sets
  • Types of data: at rest, in motion, and in use

📶 Wireless Business Technologies

  • RFID: Tags for identifying and tracking items
  • GPS: Satellite tracking for location and speed
  • GIS: Mapping tools for visualization and analysis

💬 Social Media

  • Internet-based platforms for creating and sharing content
  • Difference between social media and SNS: content sharing vs. social connection
  • Types: Blogs, collaborative projects (e.g., Wikipedia), SNS, content communities, virtual worlds

📉 Social Media Fatigue

  • Users overwhelmed by too much content, friends, or platform switching
  • Dunbar’s Number: 150 stable relationships

🔐 Privacy Fatigue

  • Consent, security, and breach fatigue
  • Users stop reading policies or caring about breaches

📰 News in Social Media

  • Friends and opinion leaders shape how people trust and seek news
  • Filter Bubble: Algorithmic content curation reinforces bias
