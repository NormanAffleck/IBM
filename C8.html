üë• Groups, Teams, and Organizational Effectiveness

  • Group: Two or more people interacting to achieve goals or needs.
  • Team: A group working intensely to achieve a common goal.
  • Benefits of groups/teams: improved performance, responsiveness, innovation, and employee motivation.

‚öôÔ∏è Synergy in Performance

  • Collaboration increases idea generation and problem-solving capacity.
  • Groups bring varied knowledge and can tackle complex tasks together.

üòä Group Motivation

  • Teamwork increases motivation and job satisfaction.
  • Members feel the impact of their contribution and benefit from social interaction.

üìÇ Types of Groups and Teams

  • Formal Group: Created by management to achieve goals.
  • Command Group: Employees reporting to the same supervisor.
  • Task Group: Formed to complete specific tasks; may be cross-functional.
  • Virtual Team: Operates via technology without physical meetings.
  • Informal Group: Formed naturally among employees.
  • Friendship Group: Based on social relationships.
  • Interest Group: Organized around shared interests or goals.

üîÑ Group Dynamics

  • Small groups are easier to coordinate and foster stronger motivation and information sharing.
  • Division of Labor: Assigning tasks to individuals based on job roles.

üìã Group Roles and Norms

  • Group Role: Expected behavior based on a group position.
  • Role Making: Expanding a role by taking on more responsibility.
  • Group Norms: Shared guidelines or rules that guide group behavior.

üé≠ Conformity vs. Deviance

  • Conformity occurs to earn rewards or imitate respected members.
  • Balanced conformity and deviance lead to high performance.

üß† Group Decision Making

  • Groupthink: Faulty decisions due to pressure to conform.

ü§ù Group Cohesiveness

  • Refers to member attraction and unity.
  • Leads to higher participation, stronger norms, and goal focus.

üí§ Social Loafing

  • People may exert less effort in groups than alone.
  • To reduce: make individual contributions identifiable, emphasize importance, and increase group cohesiveness.
